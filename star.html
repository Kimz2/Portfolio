Overview


Adopt a star is a nonprofit organization that raises funds by allowing users to purchase a star to dedicate to themselves or someone else in support of scientific research. The proceeds go to a team of scientists who analyze data from telescopes searching for habitable earth-like exoplanets outside of our solar system. Our objectives with the redesign were to provide clarity in information and the mission statement of the non-profit. We wanted to encourage users to donate to this cause by designing a delightful visual experience.

Problem

Users had difficulty locating and deciphering the mission statement, leading to a lack of motivation for donating to the cause. The descriptions of the star options are perplexing for average users that want to know the distinction between the choices. Not only does the process of adopting a star lack clear instructions and descriptions, but the visual layout also adds more challenges for readability. The website also lacked adequate feedback and simple navigation buttons.

Solution

Our aim was to redesign a refined interface with information that's easy to grasp. We wanted eliminate confusion and create an effortless experience that elicits an incentive to donate by piquing the interest of those who are unfamiliar with astronomy. We wanted to achieve this goal by using engaging visuals and simple terminology. Our success can be defined by an increase in donations from new users and a rise in recurring contributions.

Aesthetics


I utilized pops of neon colors to contrast the dark UI. I also added a slight glow effect for a futuristic, cosmic vibe. Experimenting with a dark interface proved to have it’s challenges. To avoid a cluttered feeling, we had to keep pictures, colors, and visuals to a minimum. We emphasized negative-space, microinteractions, and subtle animations to create an engaging experience for the user.

With More Time...

If we had more time, we would've worked with a more advanced prototyping tool. Due to the aggressive deadline, we were unable to implement a lot of the stuff we had learned. We felt that we didn’t have enough time to implement our vision fully because we would have to work with new tools. Our original plan was to have everything on the same page but Figma’s capabilities did not extend that far.
